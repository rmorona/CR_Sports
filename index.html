Problem Statement and Motivation


The FoWa Challenge strives to encourage students and faculty members on the Phillips Andover campus to reduce food waste.

While malnutrition is a leading issue in India, the United States is facing the increasing issue of obesity and food waste. In the United States, about 40 percent of the total food supply is wasted, with the PA community contributing about 390lbs of food each day.

As a solution to these issues, the FoWa Challenge provides a win-win situation to reducing food waste on campus. For each clean plate placed on a food carousel during dinner of April 14, we will charge sponsors by a value they decide. Therefore, greater the number of clean plates, the greater food security and availability at two schools for the blind in less-developed areas. Less food waste will also save the cafeteria huge expenses for uneaten food and allow them to purchase more expensive foods, such as blueberries and avocados, which students often ask for.
